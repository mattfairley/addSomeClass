Add Some Class.js

Make your javascript a little classier

Ever wish your website had a bit more of an aristocratic flair?

Now jQuery's .addClass function can do what it really should do: Add some class. No more slumming it with the working class websites of the internet. Set your site apart


$('#this-button').on('click'), function(){
	$('#unclassy-element').addClass('this-should-be-classier')
}

Dull images?

addSomeClass.js can also add a more dignified touch to an image, turning your stock photography of business meetings into something a little more debonair.


$('#this-button').on('click', function(){
	$('#unclassy-image').addClass('make-it-classy')
});

So dull.

Not enough for you?

Add some class has a full suite of options to make sure your site stays in the upper crust of society.

Just pass through some options (see the documentation on the github page)


$('this-boring-block-of-code').addClass('much-classier-please'{
	hat: 'tophat',
	monocle: 'true',
	bowtie: 'true',
	mustache: 'true',
	hatsize: '150'
})

Usage:

  1. Include addSomeClass.js on your page
    <script src="addSomeClass.js"></script>
  2. Call jQuery's regular addClass() method on an element:
    $('div').addClass('example');
  3. Add some options if you want:
    $('div').addClass('example', {fanciest: true});

Get your non-classy hands on it at github.com/mattfairley and bring some class to your sites.
